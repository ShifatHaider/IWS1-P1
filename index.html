ABOUT

I am Shifat Haider Al - Amin (Shifu), a 3rd year PhD student at Computer & Information Science Department at UMass Lowell . I am working under the supervision of Prof. Ruizhe Ma . My research focuses on Applied Machine Learning, Time Series Data, Multivariate Time Series, Computer Vision, and Data Science. I leverage statistical analysis and machine learning models to gain deeper insights into health sensor data and related applications.

Before joining UMass Lowell, I completed my M.S. in Mathematics (Computational) at Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET). My thesis, titled “Enumeration, Classification, and Counting of Spanning Trees in Plane 3-Trees”, was completed under the supervision of Prof. Dr. Abdul Hakim Khan and Prof. Dr. Saidur Rahman . This work was a joint research collaboration between the Department of Mathematics and the Department of Computer Science at BUET, focusing on Graph Theory and Algorithms, supported by Graph Drawing and Information Visiulization Laboratory . Prior to join at BUET, I accomplished my Baccalaureate Degree in Mathematics from University of Dhaka.

Beyond academia, I am actively involved in community and volunteer organizations, including Jaago Foundation ,Volunteer for Bangladesh, Save the Children in Bangladesh,, and Cumilla Cyclists .

RESEARCH

My research focuses on multivariate time-series modeling, imputation, and machine learning to improve insights from health and sensor data. I apply statistical analysis, signal processing, and AI-driven models to build robust pipelines, recover missing values, and develop reliable predictors for risk, early warnings, and anomaly detection. I also integrate multiple data types—such as wearable streams and images—and use large language models for data cleaning, weak labeling, and interpretability. Beyond this, I enjoy exploring broader ML topics and turning ideas into practical tools and systems that address real-world challenges and create meaningful impact.

    Volunteer — Save the Children in Bangladesh

    Category: Education

    Project “Bridging the Gap” is a global initiative in 80+ countries that amplifies the untold stories of urban and rural children. I served as 'Core Volunteer'” on the Bangladesh chapter for the ”Every Last Child campaign (#EveryLastChild). From a national pool, 23 of us were assigned to Baniachong—an area submerged in water for nearly seven months each year. In the first phase, 50 young volunteers (selected from 1,000+ applicants) completed an orientation on Save the Children and the campaign. We then split into two field teams: one to the Rayerbazar urban slum in Dhaka, and another to the remote village of Badalpur (Baniachong) in the northeast. Badalpur is flood-prone, 34 km from the nearest town, with roads cut off in the monsoon—boats are the only transport and travel can take four hours. Through community dialogues we documented barriers to schooling and healthcare and focused our storytelling on maternal mortality, newborn safety, education access, and sanitation awareness.
